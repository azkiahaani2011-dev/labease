## What Is a Full Body Checkup?

A **Full Body Checkup** (also called a Comprehensive Health Package or Master Health Checkup) is a bundle of blood and urine tests that covers all major organ systems and health markers in a single visit. Instead of booking individual tests, you get a complete health picture in one go.

Think of it as a **annual service for your body** — just as you service your car every year, your body deserves the same preventive attention.

## What Tests Are Included in a Full Body Checkup?

A standard full body checkup on LabEase includes tests across these categories:

### Blood Health
- Complete Blood Count (CBC) — checks RBC, WBC, platelets, haemoglobin
- ESR (Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate)
- Peripheral Blood Smear

### Diabetes
- Blood Sugar Fasting
- Blood Sugar Post-Prandial (PP)
- HbA1c (3-month average blood sugar)
- Insulin Fasting

### Heart & Cholesterol
- Lipid Profile (Total Cholesterol, LDL, HDL, Triglycerides, VLDL)
- hs-CRP (inflammation marker)
- Homocysteine

### Liver Health
- Liver Function Test / LFT (SGOT, SGPT, Bilirubin, Albumin, ALP)

### Kidney Health
- Kidney Function Test / KFT (Creatinine, Urea, Uric Acid, eGFR)

### Thyroid
- TSH, Free T3, Free T4

### Vitamins & Minerals
- Vitamin D (25-OH)
- Vitamin B12
- Calcium
- Iron Studies (Serum Iron, Ferritin, TIBC)

### Urine Analysis
- Urine Routine & Microscopy

### Hormones (Premium Packages)
- Testosterone (men)
- FSH, LH (women)

## How to Prepare for a Full Body Checkup

**10–12 hours before:**
- Fast completely (no food, no drinks except water)
- Avoid alcohol for 24 hours before

**Morning of the test:**
- Drink 2–3 glasses of plain water (helps blood draw)
- Take your regular medications only if your doctor says so
- Wear a loose-sleeved top for easy blood collection
- Bring your previous test reports (for comparison)

**Urine sample:**
- Collect the first-morning urine sample in the container provided
- Do not collect urine sample more than 2 hours before collection

## Understanding Your Full Body Checkup Report

Your report will have many values. Here is a simple way to read it:

1. **Green / Normal** — value is within the healthy range. Great.
2. **Yellow / Borderline** — slightly outside normal. Worth watching. Discuss with doctor.
3. **Red / Abnormal** — needs attention. See your doctor soon.

**Key numbers to track year on year:**
- Haemoglobin
- Fasting Blood Sugar & HbA1c
- Total Cholesterol & LDL
- Creatinine (kidney marker)
- SGPT (liver marker)
- TSH
- Vitamin D & B12

> ⚠️ Lab reports identify markers — they do not diagnose diseases. Always consult a qualified doctor to interpret your full body checkup report.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### What is included in a full body checkup?
A full body checkup typically includes blood count (CBC), blood sugar, HbA1c, lipid profile (cholesterol), liver function test, kidney function test, thyroid (TSH), vitamin D, vitamin B12, and urine test. Premium packages may include hormones, iron studies, and cardiac markers. Exact tests vary by package.

### How much does a full body checkup cost in India?
Full body checkup prices in India range from ₹599 to ₹3,000+ depending on the number of tests included. LabEase offers packages starting from ₹999 with 50+ tests, home collection, and NABL-certified lab analysis.

### Is fasting required for full body checkup?
Yes. Most full body checkups require **10–12 hours of fasting** because they include fasting blood sugar and lipid profile tests. Drink plain water during the fasting period. Morning slots (6–9 AM) are ideal.

### How often should I get a full body checkup?
Adults under 40 should get a full body checkup **once a year**. Above 40, every 6 months is recommended. If you have a chronic condition like diabetes or hypertension, follow your doctor's advice on frequency.

### Can I get a full body checkup done at home?
Yes. LabEase offers complete home sample collection for full body checkup packages. A phlebotomist visits your home at your chosen time, collects blood and urine samples, and you receive your digital report the same day or next day.

### Which full body checkup package is best?
For most healthy adults aged 25–40, the **Standard Full Body Checkup (50+ tests)** is ideal. Above 40, or if you have a family history of disease, choose the **Comprehensive package (70+ tests)**. Premium packages add hormonal tests and doctor consultations.

### What should I eat after a full body checkup?
After the blood draw, you can eat normally. Start with a light meal — fruit, toast, or a small breakfast. Avoid heavy or oily food right after fasting. Stay well hydrated.

### Can full body checkup detect cancer?
Routine full body checkups include general cancer markers in some premium packages (PSA for prostate, CA-125 for ovarian). However, cancer diagnosis requires specific tests, biopsies, and imaging — not a standard blood panel. If you have symptoms or family history, discuss targeted cancer screening with your doctor.

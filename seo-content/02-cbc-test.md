## What Is a CBC Test?

A **Complete Blood Count (CBC)** is a blood test that measures the three main types of cells in your blood:

- **Red Blood Cells (RBC)** — carry oxygen throughout your body
- **White Blood Cells (WBC)** — fight infections and disease
- **Platelets** — help your blood clot when you have a wound

The CBC gives your doctor a snapshot of your overall health and helps detect a wide range of conditions — from anaemia and infections to blood disorders.

---

## What Does a CBC Test Measure?

A standard CBC test measures the following parameters:

| Parameter | What It Measures | Normal Range (Adults) |
|---|---|---|
| Haemoglobin (Hb) | Oxygen-carrying capacity | Men: 13–17 g/dL, Women: 12–15 g/dL |
| RBC Count | Number of red blood cells | 4.5–5.9 million/µL |
| WBC Count | Number of white blood cells | 4,000–11,000/µL |
| Platelet Count | Blood clotting cells | 1.5–4.0 lakh/µL |
| Haematocrit (HCT) | % of blood that is RBCs | Men: 41–53%, Women: 36–46% |
| MCV | Size of red blood cells | 80–100 fL |
| MCH | Haemoglobin per RBC | 27–33 pg |
| MCHC | Haemoglobin concentration | 32–36 g/dL |
| Neutrophils | Type of WBC (infection fighter) | 40–70% |
| Lymphocytes | Type of WBC (immune cells) | 20–40% |
| Eosinophils | Type of WBC (allergy/parasite) | 1–6% |

> ⚠️ Normal ranges may vary slightly between labs and age groups. Always consult your doctor to interpret your results.

## Why Do Doctors Order a CBC Test?

Your doctor may recommend a CBC test for many reasons:

### To Diagnose Conditions
- **Anaemia** — low haemoglobin or RBC count
- **Infections** — high WBC count often indicates bacterial or viral infection
- **Dengue** — low platelet count is a key sign
- **Blood disorders** — leukaemia, thalassaemia, polycythaemia
- **Immune system problems** — abnormal WBC patterns

### As Part of Routine Health Checkups
A CBC is included in almost every annual health checkup because it screens for so many conditions at once.

### To Monitor Ongoing Treatment
- Chemotherapy patients — to track WBC and platelet levels
- Anaemia patients — to check if iron supplements are working
- Infection patients — to see if antibiotics are effective

## CBC Test Normal Range – Quick Reference

**Haemoglobin (the most important value most people check):**

| Group | Normal Range |
|---|---|
| Adult Men | 13.0–17.0 g/dL |
| Adult Women | 12.0–15.0 g/dL |
| Children (6–12 yrs) | 11.5–15.5 g/dL |
| Pregnant Women | ≥11.0 g/dL |

**Platelet Count:**
- Normal: 1.5–4.0 lakh/µL (150,000–400,000/µL)
- Low (Thrombocytopenia): <1.5 lakh — can be a sign of dengue, ITP, or liver disease
- High (Thrombocytosis): >4.0 lakh — may indicate infection or iron deficiency

**WBC Count:**
- Normal: 4,000–11,000 cells/µL
- High: Possible infection, inflammation, or leukaemia
- Low: Possible viral illness, autoimmune disease, or medication effect

## What Happens If My CBC Results Are Abnormal?

Abnormal results do **not** automatically mean you are seriously ill. Many factors can temporarily affect your CBC — dehydration, a recent infection, stress, or even time of day. Your doctor will:

1. Compare results with your symptoms
2. Look at your medical history
3. Possibly repeat the test or order additional tests

> Never self-diagnose based on a CBC report. Always consult a qualified doctor.

---

## Does a CBC Test Require Fasting?

**No.** A CBC test does not require fasting. You can eat and drink normally before the test.

However, if your doctor has ordered other tests along with CBC (such as blood sugar or lipid profile), you may need to fast for those specific tests.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### What is CBC test used for?
CBC (Complete Blood Count) is used to check your overall blood health. It helps detect anaemia, infections, blood disorders, dengue, and many other conditions. It is one of the first tests doctors order during any health evaluation.

### Is fasting required for CBC blood test?
No, fasting is not required for a CBC test. You can eat and drink normally. If other tests are ordered along with CBC, check with your doctor about fasting requirements.

### What is the normal range of CBC in adults?
Key normal values: Haemoglobin (13–17 g/dL for men, 12–15 g/dL for women), WBC (4,000–11,000/µL), Platelets (1.5–4.0 lakh/µL). Ranges vary slightly between labs.

### How long does it take to get CBC test results?
CBC results are usually ready within **2–6 hours**. LabEase delivers reports digitally, often on the same day.

### What does low haemoglobin in CBC mean?
Low haemoglobin (below 12 g/dL in women or 13 g/dL in men) indicates anaemia. This can be caused by iron deficiency, vitamin B12 deficiency, blood loss, or chronic disease. Consult your doctor for treatment.

### What does high WBC count mean?
A high WBC count (above 11,000/µL) usually indicates the body is fighting an infection, inflammation, or stress. In some cases it may suggest blood disorders. Your doctor will interpret it alongside your symptoms.

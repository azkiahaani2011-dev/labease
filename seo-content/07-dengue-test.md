## What Is Dengue Fever?

Dengue fever is a viral infection spread by the bite of the **Aedes aegypti mosquito** — typically identified by its black and white stripes. It is NOT spread from person to person.

There are **4 types (serotypes)** of dengue virus (DENV-1, 2, 3, 4). A second infection with a different serotype can cause **Severe Dengue (Dengue Haemorrhagic Fever)** — a life-threatening condition.

## Types of Dengue Tests

There are three main types of dengue blood tests:

| Test | What It Detects | Best Time to Test | Report Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **NS1 Antigen** | Dengue virus protein (early infection) | Day 1–5 of fever | 4–6 hours |
| **Dengue IgM Antibody** | Immune response (recent infection) | Day 4–14 of fever | Same day |
| **Dengue IgG Antibody** | Past infection or second infection | Day 7+ of fever | Same day |
| **Dengue NS1 + IgM + IgG Combo** | All three together | Any time during fever | Same day |

### Which Dengue Test Should You Book?

- **Within the first 5 days of fever** → Book **Dengue NS1 Antigen** test — most accurate in early infection
- **After 5 days of fever** → Book **Dengue IgM** or the **Combo (NS1 + IgM + IgG)** test
- **Unsure when fever started** → Book the **Dengue Combo Test** — covers all stages

### NS1 Antigen Result
| Result | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Negative** | Dengue NS1 protein not detected — unlikely dengue if tested in first 5 days |
| **Positive** | Dengue virus protein detected — confirm with IgM; start treatment |

### IgM Antibody Result
| Result | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Negative** | No current dengue infection (or too early to detect) |
| **Positive** | Active or recent dengue infection (within last 2–3 months) |

### IgG Antibody Result
| Result | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Negative** | No prior dengue exposure |
| **Positive** | Past dengue infection OR second infection (higher risk of severe dengue) |

> ⚠️ A negative NS1 result in the first 5 days does not completely rule out dengue. If symptoms continue, repeat the test with IgM after day 5.

## Dengue Symptoms – When Should You Get Tested?

Get tested **immediately** if you have:

### Classic Dengue Symptoms
- Sudden high fever (38.5°C – 40°C or 101°F – 104°F)
- Severe headache, especially behind the eyes
- Muscle and joint pain ("breakbone fever")
- Rash on skin (appears 3–5 days after fever starts)
- Nausea and vomiting
- Fatigue and weakness

### Warning Signs of Severe Dengue (Go to Hospital Immediately)
- Severe abdominal pain
- Persistent vomiting (3+ times in 24 hours)
- Bleeding from nose or gums
- Blood in urine or stools (black, tarry stools)
- Rapid breathing
- Extreme fatigue or restlessness
- Falling platelet count on repeat tests

**Test early. Don't wait for a rash or dropping platelets.**

## What Happens to Platelets in Dengue?

Dengue causes the platelet count (measured in CBC) to drop. This is one of the most dangerous aspects of dengue.

| Platelet Count | Status |
|---|---|
| 1.5–4.0 lakh/µL | Normal |
| 1.0–1.5 lakh/µL | Mild drop — monitor at home with daily CBC |
| 0.5–1.0 lakh/µL | Moderate drop — close medical supervision required |
| Below 0.5 lakh (50,000/µL) | Severe — hospitalisation often needed |
| Below 0.2 lakh (20,000/µL) | Critical — risk of spontaneous bleeding |

When dengue is detected, doctors usually order a **CBC test daily** to monitor platelet count and guide treatment decisions.

## Dengue Treatment – What to Do at Home

There is **no specific antiviral drug for dengue**. Treatment is supportive:

### Do:
- ✅ Rest completely
- ✅ Drink plenty of fluids (ORS, coconut water, plain water, fresh lime water)
- ✅ Take **paracetamol** for fever and pain
- ✅ Monitor platelet count daily with a CBC test
- ✅ Watch for warning signs

### Do NOT:
- ❌ Take aspirin or ibuprofen (NSAIDs) — they increase bleeding risk
- ❌ Take antibiotics — dengue is viral, antibiotics don't work
- ❌ Self-medicate with steroids
- ❌ Ignore warning signs (see above)

## Dengue Prevention

- Use mosquito repellent (DEET-based) on exposed skin
- Wear full-sleeve clothing, especially at dawn and dusk
- Use mosquito nets while sleeping
- Remove standing water around your home (coolers, flower pots, tyres, containers)
- Install mosquito screens on windows and doors
- The Aedes mosquito breeds in **clean stagnant water** — even small amounts

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### Which dengue test is most accurate?
The **Dengue NS1 Antigen test** is most accurate in the first 5 days of fever. After day 5, the **Dengue IgM antibody test** is more reliable. The **Combo test (NS1 + IgM + IgG)** is the most comprehensive and covers all stages.

### When should I get a dengue test?
Get tested within the **first 1–3 days of fever** for best results. NS1 is accurate in the first 5 days. Don't wait for a rash or platelet drop — early detection leads to better outcomes.

### What is a safe platelet count in dengue?
A platelet count above 1.0 lakh (100,000/µL) is generally manageable at home with monitoring. Below 0.5 lakh (50,000/µL), close medical supervision is required. Below 0.2 lakh (20,000/µL) may require hospitalisation and platelet transfusion.

### Can dengue be treated at home?
Mild dengue (no warning signs, stable platelet count) can be managed at home with rest, fluids, and paracetamol. However, daily platelet monitoring via CBC is essential. Any warning signs (severe pain, bleeding, extreme fatigue) require immediate hospitalisation.

### How long does dengue fever last?
Dengue fever typically lasts **7–10 days**. Fever usually breaks by day 5–7. Fatigue and weakness can persist for 2–4 weeks after recovery.

### Is a negative NS1 test conclusive?
Not always. NS1 is most sensitive in the first 5 days. If fever persists beyond 5 days and NS1 is negative, a Dengue IgM test should be done. A negative result in both tests, along with no symptoms, suggests dengue is unlikely.

### Can dengue spread from person to person?
No. Dengue is spread **only by the bite of the Aedes aegypti mosquito** — not by touching, coughing, or contact with an infected person's blood in normal circumstances.

## What Is a Thyroid Profile Test?

A **Thyroid Profile Test** (also called Thyroid Function Test or TFT) measures the levels of thyroid hormones in your blood to assess how well your thyroid gland is working.

The standard Thyroid Profile measures three hormones:

| Hormone | Full Name | Where Produced | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| **TSH** | Thyroid Stimulating Hormone | Pituitary gland (brain) | Controls thyroid hormone production |
| **T3** | Triiodothyronine | Thyroid gland | Active thyroid hormone — controls metabolism |
| **T4** | Thyroxine | Thyroid gland | Main thyroid hormone — converted to T3 in the body |

Most doctors first order **TSH only** as a screening test. If TSH is abnormal, they then order the full T3, T4, TSH panel.

## Thyroid Test Normal Range

### TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)
| Result | Range | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| Normal | 0.4 – 4.0 mIU/L | Thyroid is functioning normally |
| Low TSH | < 0.4 mIU/L | Possible **hyperthyroidism** (overactive thyroid) |
| High TSH | > 4.0 mIU/L | Possible **hypothyroidism** (underactive thyroid) |

### T3 (Free T3)
| Result | Normal Range |
|---|---|
| Free T3 | 2.3 – 4.2 pg/mL |

### T4 (Free T4)
| Result | Normal Range |
|---|---|
| Free T4 | 0.8 – 1.8 ng/dL |

> ⚠️ Reference ranges vary slightly between labs. Always consult your doctor to interpret your results in the context of your symptoms and medical history.

## Signs You May Need a Thyroid Test

### Signs of Hypothyroidism (Low Thyroid)
- Unexplained weight gain
- Constant fatigue and weakness
- Feeling cold all the time
- Dry skin and hair loss
- Constipation
- Depression or low mood
- Slow heart rate
- Puffy face

### Signs of Hyperthyroidism (Overactive Thyroid)
- Unexplained weight loss
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat
- Excessive sweating
- Nervousness or anxiety
- Trembling hands
- Difficulty sleeping
- Frequent bowel movements

### Who Should Get a Thyroid Test?
- Women above 30 years (thyroid problems are 5–10x more common in women)
- Anyone with a family history of thyroid disease
- Pregnant women or those trying to conceive
- People with unexplained fatigue, weight changes, or hair loss
- Patients with diabetes or PCOS (higher risk of thyroid issues)
- Anyone on thyroid medication (for regular monitoring)

## Does a Thyroid Test Require Fasting?

**No.** The Thyroid Profile test does not require fasting. You can eat and drink normally before the test.

However, if you take thyroid medication (like Levothyroxine/Eltroxin), it is best to **take the blood test before your morning dose**. This gives the most accurate reading of your base TSH level.

## Hypothyroidism vs Hyperthyroidism – Key Differences

| Feature | Hypothyroidism | Hyperthyroidism |
|---|---|---|
| TSH Level | High (>4.0) | Low (<0.4) |
| T3/T4 Levels | Low | High |
| Weight | Gain | Loss |
| Heartrate | Slow | Fast |
| Energy | Fatigue | Restlessness |
| Common Cause | Hashimoto's disease, iodine deficiency | Graves' disease, toxic goitre |
| Treatment | Thyroxine tablets | Anti-thyroid drugs, radioiodine |

## Thyroid and Pregnancy – Why It Matters

Thyroid hormones are critical during pregnancy. Uncontrolled thyroid disease during pregnancy can cause:
- Miscarriage or preterm birth
- Low birth weight
- Developmental problems in the baby

The Indian Thyroid Society recommends TSH screening for **all pregnant women in the first trimester**.

If you are pregnant or planning a pregnancy, get your thyroid tested.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### What is the difference between TSH and T3 T4 thyroid test?
TSH is the first test done to screen for thyroid problems. T3 and T4 are the actual thyroid hormones. If TSH is abnormal, doctors order T3 and T4 to understand whether the thyroid is over- or under-active, and by how much.

### What is the normal TSH level for a woman?
Normal TSH for adult women is 0.4–4.0 mIU/L. For pregnant women, the recommended range is stricter: 0.1–2.5 mIU/L in the first trimester. Post-menopause, slightly higher TSH values may be acceptable.

### Can I take my thyroid medicine before the test?
If you are on thyroid medication, take the blood test **before** your morning dose for the most accurate TSH reading. Discuss this with your doctor.

### Is TSH test done fasting?
No fasting is required for thyroid tests. You can eat and drink normally before the test.

### What does high TSH mean?
A high TSH (above 4.0 mIU/L) usually means your thyroid is underactive (hypothyroidism). Your pituitary gland is sending extra signals to the thyroid to work harder. Treatment typically involves thyroxine tablets prescribed by a doctor.

### What does low TSH mean?
A low TSH (below 0.4 mIU/L) usually means your thyroid is overactive (hyperthyroidism). Consult an endocrinologist for diagnosis and treatment options.

### How often should I get a thyroid test?
If you have a diagnosed thyroid condition, your doctor will advise monitoring every 3–6 months. For general health screening, once a year is recommended, especially for women above 30.

### Can thyroid problems cause weight gain?
Yes. Hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid) causes the metabolism to slow down, leading to unexplained weight gain, fatigue, and cold intolerance. Treating the thyroid condition usually helps normalize weight over time.
